Who developed the theory of evolution?

Born on February 12, 1809, Charles Robert Darwin changed the world. He originally wanted to study medicine and become a doctor like his family before him. But when he was offered a job as a natural scientist on HMS Beagle in 1831 he jumped at the chance. As planned, he spent most of his time on land investigating geology. The fossils he found on the beaches were evidence of animals who had lived many thousands or even millions of years before. But he developed his theory of evolution when HMS Beagle stopped at the Galapagos Islands. He realised that each island had its own type of finch that were similar but different in important ways. He believed that they were related but had evolved to adapt to their different environments.
